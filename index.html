About me

Born and raised in three different continents to wanderlust parents, perhaps that is when I was infused with world view and the urge to travel. While growing up in different countries, I have learned four languages along the way: English, Mandarin, Cantonese, and Taiwanese.

Besides the human languages, I have taught myself more than half a dozen computer programming languages. Ever since tapping on the keyboard of my father's i286 computer in the 90s, I have been drawn to the leading edge of technology. I have built my own desktop computer, programmed my car's on-board diagnostic system, and wrote mobile applications to help with everyday life. All of which were accomplished by lots of self-learning.

The skills I have taught myself goes on to include music, photography, tinkering with various machines, and wilderness survival skills. To put my outdoor knowledge to good use, I became an executive of the University of Ottawa Outdoors Club for several years and lead numerous trips. Not only was I able to hone my skills as an event organizer, I was able to satisfy my need to travel, playing guitar by the campfire, and capture the memorable moments in the form of photography. Most importantly, enjoying time with friends and doing the things we love, isn't that what life is all about?

Travel

"Not all who wander are lost."

— J. R. R. Tolkien



My parents are adventurers! Even though they both came from poor families, they worked hard to see the world. After having children, they did not show any signs of slowing down.

Since my childhood, my family and I had many opportunities to travel, study, and live abroad. The numerous experiences I have lead me to my own adventures, from watching the sunrise over the rolling hills to photographing the curvature of the earth by the Ocean. Each trip allows me to grow and mature, as well as to learn from my mistakes. As there are always more places to see and more cultures to enjoy, I will continue to explore the world.

Saturday Mechanic


I suppose it all started with building LEGO robots and disassembling radios. Then, during university, I found out the secondhand car I bought for my road trip was a lemon. With no experience working under the hood and a potential repair bill of tens of thousands, I set to work with a $25 Canadian Tire repair manual and a discount toolbox. As it turns out, maintenances and repairs are quite simple provided the correct tools, parts, and instructions are available. After invested lots of time and gumption, I not only kept my car running for 6 years and 100,000km, but also a road trip across North America and gained the skills that allows me to make machines run smoothly.


Since I moved to the city, I sold my car as cars are no longer the most convenient form of transportation. In pursuit of more monkey wrenching, I have took my skills to build bicycles. Volunteering at the University of Ottawa Bicycle Co-op, we work on bicycles of all shapes and forms. We help University students and community members repair their bikes as well as building bikes from donated parts. Depending on the parts we acquire, we assemble them and donate them back to the community. I have even competed in a triathlon with a bicycle built entirely out of scrap parts!


Photography

"Take nothing but pictures. Leave nothing but footprints."

— Motto of the Baltimore Grotto



Photography is a great way to preserve memories. After taking many photos with point and shoot cameras, a friend and I decided to learn photography from scratch one university summer. To go back basics, we used film SLR cameras. For me, it was a 1990 Minolta X-9, without the fancy digital wizardry. Controlling only the focus, aperture, and shutter speed, it wasn't long before the rolls of film piled up. With the rising film development cost and cheaper digital camera available, it was time to graduate to digital cameras.

Moving forward, I picked up a Canon T3 during one boxing day sale, I can now take more than 36 photos at a time! Although DSLRs are full of digital aids, I still hold true to the basics of photography. I refrain from editing photos, once the shutter opens and closes, the moment is preserved and should stay unaltered. So far, I have travelled to many picturesque locations and attempted to capture these pictures, a few of them are shown on this website.



Music

"Music expresses that which cannot be said and on which it is impossible to be silent."

— Victor Hugo




Most children were forced to play the piano when they were young, I was no different. Although I didn't get far into playing any particular instrument, I did learn the basics of music.


Years later, driven by my own interest, I have taught myself to play the guitar, ukulele, and saxophone. Even though I won't be playing at any concerts anytime soon, it is enough for a sing along at the campfire.


Endurance sports

"You are never closer to achieving your goals than the moment before you quit."

— Mark Black



Some people find jogging boring; to me, it is just a process to keep myself healthy and fit. I joined the Iron Knights Half-marathon Running Club back in high school and have not stopped running. We trained throughout the year, even in the -20°C winter days. Even though the training processes are gruelling, the feeling when crossing a finish line while the crowd cheers you on is indescribable.

Seeking that endorphin high, I have since completed over 20 half-marathons. In recent years, I have included triathlons and other fun runs with obstacle courses. Aside from keeping me fit, endurance sports have shaped me with discipline and taught me never to give up.
